Unit 0.1: Your First Steps in Excel

▼
  • 1 Introduction for Complete Beginners

    • Hello! Welcome to Excel!

    If you're new to Excel, don't worry! This guide will walk you through EVERYTHING like you've never seen Excel before. We'll use your workbooks to learn together.

    Important: This guide assumes you have the "Excel 365 Intro Example File Complete.xlsx" file. If you don't have it, you can still follow along with any Excel file.
  • 2 Opening Your First Excel File

    • Let's Open Excel

    Step 1: Opening Your First Excel File

    1. Find the Excel file on your computer (look for "Excel 365 Intro Example File Complete.xlsx")
    2. Double-click the file to open it
    3. You'll see something like this:
    ┌─────────────────────────────────┐ │ ╭─────────────────────────────╮ │ │ │ Excel │ │ │ │ [Your file name here] │ │ │ ╰─────────────────────────────╯ │ │ │ │ A bunch of letters across top │ │ (A B C D E F G...) │ │ │ │ Numbers going down (1 2 3 4...) │ │ │ │ Little boxes with information │ │ │ │ Tabs at bottom (2023, 2024...) │ └─────────────────────────────────┘
    DON'T PANIC! This is normal. Let's break it down.
  • 3 Understanding the Excel Screen

    • Look at Your Screen - Find These Parts:

    A. Top Part (Ribbon): [File] [Home] [Insert] [Page Layout] [Formulas] [Data] [Review] [View] [Help] B. Big Grid Area (Worksheet): Columns: Letters A, B, C, D... (goes across) Rows: Numbers 1, 2, 3, 4... (goes down) Cells: Where column and row meet (like A1, B2, C3) C. Bottom Part (Sheet Tabs): Sheet1 Sheet2 Sheet3 (click these to change pages) In your file: 2023 2024 2025 2026 COMMON FUNCTIONS D. Formula Bar (Just Below Ribbon): This shows what's INSIDE a cell ┌─────────────────────┐ │ fx │ │ ← This is the formula bar └─────────────────────┘
    Remember: The Ribbon is like a menu. We'll mostly use "Home" for now.

  • 2 Simple Math in Excel

    • Excel Can Do Math FOR You! Let's Try:

    On the 2023 sheet, look at cell K4 (Total Items Sold for Original Coffee)

    Step 1: Understanding What's Already There:

    1. Click on cell K4
    2. Look up at the formula bar
    3. It says: =SUM(G4:J4)

    What does this mean?

    = means "calculate something"

    SUM means "add things up"

    (G4:J4) means "from cell G4 to cell J4"

    Step 2: Let's Check the Numbers:

    Look at cells G4, H4, I4, J4:

    • G4: 453 (Qtr. 1 sales)
    • H4: 563 (Qtr. 2 sales)
    • I4: 1253 (Qtr. 3 sales)
    • J4: 1515 (Qtr. 4 sales)

    Total: 453 + 563 + 1253 + 1515 = 3784

    Look at K4 - it says 3784!

    Step 3: Let's Make Our Own Simple Calculation:

    Find an empty spot on your sheet (maybe cell N15)

    1. Click on N15
    2. Type: =5+3
    3. Press ENTER
    4. Excel shows: 8

    You just did math in Excel!

    Another Example:

    1. Click on N16
    2. Type: =10*2 (The * means multiply)
    3. Press ENTER
    4. Excel shows: 20
  • 3 Following the Coffee Shop Report

    • Let's Trace Through the Calculations Together:

    Part 1: Total Items Sold (Column K)

    For Original Coffee (row 4):

    1. Excel adds: Q1 + Q2 + Q3 + Q4
    2. Formula: =SUM(G4:J4)
    3. Result: 3784

    For Expresso (row 5):

    1. Click K5
    2. Formula bar shows: =SUM(G5:J5)
    3. It adds: 288 + 432 + 625 + 1127 = 2472

    Part 2: Total Sales (Column L)

    For Original Coffee:

    1. Click L4
    2. Formula bar: =E4*K4
    3. This means: Price × Total Items
    4. Price in E4: 1.75
    5. Total Items in K4: 3784
    6. Calculation: 1.75 × 3784 = 6622
    7. Excel shows: 6622

    Part 3: Taxes (Column M)

    For Original Coffee:

    1. Click M4
    2. Formula bar: =$M$1*L4
    3. This means: Tax Rate × Total Sales
    4. Tax Rate in M1: 0.0725
    5. Total Sales in L4: 6622
    6. Calculation: 0.0725 × 6622 = 480.095
    7. Excel shows: 480.095
    IMPORTANT NOTICE: See the $ signs in $M$1? That means "ALWAYS use cell M1, no matter what." It's like saying "Use THIS specific cell."

Unit 0.3: Working with the Grading Sheet

▼
  • 1 Calculating Student Totals

    • Let's Switch to COMMON FUNCTIONS Sheet:

    Step 1: Click the "COMMON FUNCTIONS" tab at bottom

    Step 2: Look at the Student Data:

    You'll see:

    • Student names
    • Their scores in Science, English, History, Maths
    • Empty columns for TOTAL, AVERAGE, GRADE

    Step 3: Let's Calculate Bronnie's Total Score:

    Bronnie is in row 3:

    • Science: 21 (cell G3)
    • English: 81 (cell H3)
    • History: 62 (cell I3)
    • Maths: 49 (cell J3)

    We need to put the total in column K.

    Method 1: Typing the Formula

    1. Click on cell K3 (empty cell next to Maths score)
    2. Type exactly: =G3+H3+I3+J3
    3. Press ENTER
    4. You should see: 213 (because 21+81+62+49=213)

    Method 2: Using SUM Function (Easier!)

    1. Click on cell K3
    2. Type: =SUM( (just type this much)
    3. Now use your mouse: Click on G3
    4. Hold SHIFT key, click on J3
    5. (This selects G3, H3, I3, J3 all at once)
    6. Type: ) (close the parenthesis)
    7. Press ENTER
    8. You should see: 213

    Step 4: Copy the Formula Down:

    Now we need to do this for ALL students.

    Instead of typing each one, we COPY it!

    1. Make sure K3 is selected (has the formula)
    2. Look at the bottom-right corner of the cell
    3. See the tiny square? Hover over it until cursor becomes a +
    4. Click and HOLD the left mouse button
    5. DRAG down to about row 10
    6. Release mouse button

    Magic! All students now have their totals!

    Step 5: Check Your Work:

    1. Click on K4 (Lemmie's total)
    2. Look at formula bar - it should say: =SUM(G4:J4)

    Excel AUTOMATICALLY changed it from G3:J3 to G4:J4!

  • 2 Creating a Grading System

    • Now Let's Calculate Grades in Column M:

    We Need to Create This Rule:

    • If score is >70 → "A"
    • If score 60-70 → "B"
    • If score 50-59 → "C"
    • If score 40-49 → "D"
    • Below 40 → "Fail"

    Step 1: First, We Need Averages

    Before we can grade, we need average scores in column L.

    1. Click L3 (where Bronnie's average should go)
    2. Type: =K3/4 (Total ÷ 4 subjects)
    3. Press ENTER - shows 53.25
    4. Copy down (drag that little square down)

    Step 2: The IF Formula for Grades

    This looks complicated but we'll do it piece by piece.

    1. Click M3 (where grade will go)
    2. Type this SLOWLY:
    =IF(L3>70,"A", IF(L3>60,"B", IF(L3>50,"C", IF(L3>40,"D","Fail"))))

    What this means:

    • =IF(L3>70,"A", → If L3 is greater than 70, put "A"
    • IF(L3>60,"B", → If not A, but greater than 60, put "B"
    • IF(L3>50,"C", → If not B, but greater than 50, put "C"
    • IF(L3>40,"D","Fail")))) → If not C, but greater than 40, put "D", otherwise "Fail"

    Step 3: Press ENTER and See Result

    Bronnie's average is 53.25

    That's >50 but not >60

    So she gets a "C"

    Step 4: Copy Down

    Drag that little square down column M

    See all students get grades automatically!

    Pro Tip: The IF function is one of Excel's most powerful tools. It lets Excel make decisions based on your data!

  • 1 Working with Tables and Filters

    • Let's Open the Intermediate File:

    Step 1: Open the Second File

    1. Go to File → Open (top left)
    2. Find "Excel 365 Intermediate Example File.xlsx"
    3. Double-click to open it

    Step 2: Look at the "Creating Tables" Sheet

    You'll see employee information:

    • Emp ID, Last Name, First Name, Dept, etc.
    • Lots of data (37 employees)

    Step 3: Making This Data Easier to Read with a TABLE

    A Table in Excel is like putting your data in a nice, organized box.

    1. Click ANYWHERE in the data (like cell A5)
    2. Go to the "Insert" tab at top
    3. Click "Table" (it looks like a grid)
    4. A box pops up - click "OK"

    Magic happens!

    • Your data gets stripes (alternating colors)
    • Little arrows appear next to each column name
    • A new tab appears: "Table Design"

    Step 4: Filtering Data (Finding What You Need)

    Let's Find All Employees in the "AT" Department:

    1. Look at the "Dept" column (column D)
    2. See the little arrow ▼ next to "Dept"? Click that arrow
    3. A dropdown menu appears with: (Select All) and checkboxes for AT, AD, AC, HR, MK, MF
    4. Click (Select All) to UNCHECK everything
    5. Now CHECK only "AT"
    6. Click OK

    Result: Only 7 employees show up (those in AT department)!

    Step 5: Let's Filter More - By Building

    Now let's see only AT employees in Building 1:

    1. Click the arrow next to "Location"
    2. Uncheck (Select All)
    3. Check only "Building 1"
    4. Click OK

    Result: Only 5 employees now (AT department in Building 1)

    Step 6: Clearing Filters

    To see ALL employees again:

    1. Click the Data tab
    2. Click "Clear" (in Sort & Filter group)
    3. OR click each filter arrow and choose "Clear Filter"

  • 2 Beginner's Cheat Sheet and Next Steps

    • Top 10 Things to Remember:

    1. Click once to select a cell
    2. Double-click to edit inside a cell
    3. Formula always starts with =
    4. SUM adds things: =SUM(A1:A10)
    5. $ means "don't change this": =$A$1
    6. Drag the little square to copy formulas
    7. Filter arrows help find data
    8. Insert tab has charts and tables
    9. Home tab has formatting (colors, fonts)
    10. Save often! Ctrl + S

    Common Mistakes and Fixes:

    Mistake: Formula shows instead of result

    Fix: You forgot the = at beginning

    Mistake: #DIV/0! error

    Fix: You're dividing by zero or empty cell

    Mistake: Everything looks like #####

    Fix: Column is too narrow - make it wider

    Mistake: Can't type in cell

    Fix: You might be in "View" mode - press ESC
