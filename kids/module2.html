Effective Search Techniques
Poor Search:

"how much gravity is on the moon"

Problem: Uses natural language, may not find technical information

Better Search:

"gravitational force moon"

Why it works: Uses specific keywords that match technical content

Advanced Search Operators
Quotation Marks - for exact phrases
Example: "causes of world war II"
Finds pages with that exact phrase in order
Minus Sign - to exclude words
Example: jaguar speed -car
Excludes results about the car, focuses on the animal
Site: - search within a specific website
Example: climate change site:nasa.gov
Only shows results from NASA's website
The CRAAP Test - Source Evaluation

A method to evaluate the credibility and reliability of online sources.

C
Currency

When was it published/updated?

Is the information current enough for your topic?

R
Relevance

Does it answer your question?

Is it appropriate for your age/understanding level?

A
Authority

Who wrote it? What are their credentials?

Look for .edu, .gov, or reputable organizations

A
Accuracy

Is it supported by evidence?

Are there spelling/grammar errors? Can you verify the information elsewhere?

P
Purpose

Why was it written? To inform, sell, persuade, or entertain?

Is there bias or a hidden agenda?

Teaching Tip: Use the analogy of a food inspector checking a restaurant - they look at cleanliness (accuracy), freshness (currency), qualifications (authority), etc.

Common Questions & Troubleshooting

Student Question: "Why do I get different results than my friend when we search the same thing?"

Answer: Search engines personalize results based on your location, search history, and online behavior.

Student Question: "How can I tell if a website is trustworthy?"

Answer: Look for contact information, "About Us" page, professional design, and check the domain (.edu, .gov are generally reliable).

Issue: Too many irrelevant search results

Solution: Use more specific keywords, quotation marks for exact phrases, and the minus sign to exclude unwanted topics.

Attachments Best Practices
  • Always mention attachments in the email body
  • Double-check you've actually attached the files before sending
  • Use descriptive file names (e.g., "Science_Project_Report.docx" not "document1.docx")
  • Consider file size - compress large files or use cloud sharing links
Common Mistake: Students often write "I've attached the file" but forget to actually attach it. Teach them to make attaching files the LAST step before sending.

Benefits of Cloud Storage
Access from Anywhere - Files available on any device with internet
Automatic Backup - Protection against computer failure or loss
Easy Sharing - Send links instead of large email attachments
Collaboration - Multiple people can work on same document simultaneously
Version History - Recover previous versions of documents
Analogy: Cloud storage is like a safety deposit box at a bank - your valuables are stored securely in a professional facility rather than keeping them all at home where they could be lost or damaged.

Common Challenges & Solutions

Issue: Student forgets to attach files mentioned in email

Solution: Teach "attach then send" workflow - write email, mention attachment, THEN attach file, THEN send.

Issue: Student can't access shared Google Doc

Solution: Check sharing permissions - may need to change from "Restricted" to "Anyone with link" or specific people.

Issue: Running out of cloud storage space

Solution: Regularly clean up unnecessary files, use compression for images, consider multiple free accounts if needed.

Security Considerations

Important Security Points to Emphasize:

  • Never share login credentials with anyone
  • Use strong, unique passwords for email and cloud accounts
  • Enable two-factor authentication when available
  • Be careful what you store in the cloud - assume it could potentially be accessed by others
  • Log out of shared computers after using cloud services
Assessment & Reinforcement

Formative Assessment Questions:

Practical Assessment: Have students compose and send a professional email requesting information about a topic of interest, with proper formatting, attachment, and cloud storage link.
