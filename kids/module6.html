Password Creation Strategy
Step 1: Choose 4-5 unrelated words
Step 2: Mix uppercase and lowercase letters
Step 3: Add numbers and symbols
Step 4: Make it memorable but not personal
Step 5: Use different passwords for important accounts

Memory Technique: Create a story from your passphrase. "Blue Tiger Eats 8 Pizzas" creates a funny mental image that's easy to remember.

Phishing Scams - Recognizing the Bait
Example 1: Fake Netflix Email

"Your Netflix account is on hold! Click here to update payment."

Red Flags:

  • Creates false urgency
  • Generic greeting ("Dear User")
  • Suspicious sender address
  • Poor grammar/spelling
Example 2: Prize Scam

"You've won a $1000 gift card! Send us your details to claim it."

Red Flags:

  • Too good to be true
  • Asks for personal information
  • No prior contact or entry
  • Pressure to act quickly
Example 3: Fake Bank Alert

"Suspicious activity detected! Verify your account now."

Red Flags:

  • Uses fear and urgency
  • Asks for login credentials
  • Link goes to fake website
  • No specific account details
Link Hover Technique
Step 1: Hover your mouse over any suspicious link
Step 2: Look at the bottom-left of your browser
Step 3: Check the actual URL that appears
Step 4: Compare to the legitimate website address
Example: Link says "netflix.com" but hover shows "netf1ix-security.com"
Teaching Tip: Show real examples (safe ones) of phishing emails and demonstrate the hover technique. This visual demonstration makes the concept concrete.

Cyberbullying Awareness & Response
What is Cyberbullying?
  • Harassment through digital devices
  • Repeated, hostile behavior
  • Spreading rumors or lies
  • Exclusion from online groups
  • Impersonation or fake profiles
If You're Being Cyberbullied
  • Don't respond or retaliate
  • Save evidence (screenshots)
  • Block the person immediately
  • Report to platform and school
  • Talk to trusted adult
If You Witness Cyberbullying
  • Don't forward or like harmful content
  • Support the victim privately
  • Report the content to platform
  • Be an upstander, not bystander
  • Encourage seeking adult help

Common Challenges & Solutions

Issue: "But I have nothing to hide!"

Solution: Privacy isn't about hiding - it's about controlling your information and how it's used

Issue: "Everyone else shares everything"

Solution: You control your boundaries. What feels comfortable for others may not be right for you

Issue: Difficulty managing screen time

Solution: Use built-in screen time controls, set specific limits, create phone-free zones

Issue: Fear of missing out (FOMO)

Solution: Schedule specific times to check social media, focus on quality real-world connections

Advanced Tips for Digital Leaders

Digital Literacy: Learn to identify misinformation and biased sources

Positive Digital Citizenship: Use online platforms to spread positivity and support others

Privacy Advocacy: Understand data privacy laws and your rights

Mental Health First Aid: Learn to recognize when friends might need help

Digital Legacy: Consider what you want your long-term digital footprint to be
